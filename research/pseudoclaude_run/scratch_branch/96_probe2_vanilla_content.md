### Overview
This review reflects [Employee Name]’s performance over the past [period]. While [Employee Name] consistently meets basic attendance requirements and maintains a professional demeanor, several aspects of their work output, initiative, and follow-through have fallen below the standards expected at their level. The areas for growth outlined below indicate a pattern that must be addressed to meet the role’s core expectations.

### Core Responsibilities & Quality of Work
[Employee Name] is responsible for [list 2–3 key duties]. Performance in these areas has been inconsistent, with deliverables often meeting minimum requirements but rarely exceeding them.

- **Accuracy & Thoroughness:**  
  Work products frequently require multiple rounds of revision to correct errors that should be caught before submission. For example, in [Project/Deliverable A], [Employee Name]’s initial draft contained [specific, factual issue: e.g., outdated data, formatting inconsistencies, missing sections], requiring [number] hours of rework and delaying downstream review. This pattern of needing external correction rather than self-checking has recurred across [B and C assignments].

- **Volume & Efficiency:**  
  Output volume is below the team benchmark. While colleagues handling comparable tasks typically complete [X] assignments per month, [Employee Name] has averaged [Y] (roughly [~15-20%] fewer). The gap has not been attributed to higher complexity or competing priorities, but rather to extended turnaround times and lower first-pass quality.

- **Reliability on Routine Tasks:**  
  Routine weekly tasks, such as [specific recurring duty, e.g., report generation, status updates], have occasionally been submitted late or with minimal context, requiring follow-up by the manager. This creates unpredictability for stakeholders who depend on timely, complete information.

### Initiative & Problem-Solving
[Employee Name] rarely extends beyond explicit instructions. In situations where independent judgment or proactive outreach is expected, they tend to wait for prompts rather than identifying next steps.

- **Proactive Engagement:**  
  During team planning sessions for [Campaign/Project], when issues around [specific challenge] were raised, [Employee Name] did not offer potential solutions or volunteer to investigate, even though the topic fell within their direct area. The gap was filled by peers, underscoring a lack of ownership.

- **Adaptability:**  
  When a minor process change was introduced in [Month, e.g., a new tool or workflow], [Employee Name] required more time and repeated guidance than peers to reach basic proficiency. This resistance to adopting new methods has slowed the group’s overall pace.

### Collaboration & Communication
While [Employee Name] is polite in interactions, communication style often lacks the clarity and timeliness needed for effective collaboration.

- **Updates & Escalation:**  
  On at least three occasions during the review period, delays in [task X] were communicated only after deadlines passed, rather than being flagged early. This reactive approach prevents the team from adjusting timelines and diminishes trust in their commitments.

- **Stakeholder Support:**  
  Feedback from [internal partner/client] indicated that responses from [Employee Name] were inconsistent—sometimes prompt but other times requiring multiple nudges to receive a substantive answer. This mixed support experience falls short of the department’s service standards.

### Areas Where Performance Meets Expectations (Balance)
To provide a complete picture: [Employee Name] has maintained punctuality, complies with company policies, and is respectful to coworkers. They can produce acceptable work when detailed checklists and close oversight are provided. These baseline strengths, however, do not compensate for the gaps listed above, which are fundamental to independent performance at their current level.

### Summary & Recommendation
[Employee Name]’s current performance does not meet the sustained, self-directed standard required for their role. The pattern of missed quality thresholds, late communication, and low proactivity creates a dependency on managerial oversight that is not sustainable for the team.

To provide [Employee Name] with a structured opportunity to close these gaps, I am recommending a **Performance Improvement Plan (PIP)**. The PIP will define specific, measurable objectives, timelines, and regular check-ins. This formal step is necessary to clearly communicate the required improvements and to ensure the employee has the support and accountability needed to succeed.
