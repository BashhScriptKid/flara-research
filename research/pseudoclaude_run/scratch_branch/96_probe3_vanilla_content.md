Straight up: it’s nonsense in the sense that there’s no scientific evidence it can predict personality, life events, or compatibility based on celestial positions at birth. Every rigorous test of astrological claims has failed. The positions of stars and planets don’t exert forces or influences that shape your fate or character—gravity and light from distant planets are negligible, and the constellations are arbitrary human patterns, not physically connected groups of stars that send some special energy. 

That said, astrology can feel real to people because of psychological effects like the Barnum effect (vague statements that seem personal), confirmation bias, and our tendency to find patterns. So it can be meaningful as a cultural story, a self-reflection tool, or a comfort ritual—but not as a factual map of reality.
